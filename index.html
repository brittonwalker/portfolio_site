The New York Review of Books

In 2019 I worked with Athletics to help bring a richer experience to NYRB's forty plus years of archival content. The site was built with WordPress, Timber, Twig, ACF, React, Bootstrap, Javascript, and is now hosted on WordPress VIP. The NYRB site won a people's choice webby in 2020.

TURF

In 2022 I worked with the Athletics team to switch from WordPress to using Sanity.io and bring their new brand identity to life. This project uses React, Next.js, Sanity.io, and other third party tools to bring a rich experience and understanding of TURF's beautifully made felt acoustic panelling.

TKTK Theme

Building a good starter theme was an OKR for me at Athletics. Early in 2022, I set out to meet that goal and created a starter theme for developers that has some widely used tools to help get developers off the ground. This theme is bootstrapped with Timber, Twig, Barba.js, Tailwind, eslint, and the build processes to start making your next award winning website.

tktk site ↗

TKTK Components

When building the TKTK theme, I wanted to pack it with a lot of commonly used components with minimal styling and functionality that would just work. I've decided to keep the theme lean and let this be a repository of components to show you how to get there, complete with code snippets. My inspiration came from seeing other Tailwind component libraries that I was happy to provide for free so developers of any level can use or learn in their process. It's a fun ongoing project that makes me really think about the code.

More More

Athletics likes to give back at the end of each year to causes they believe in and try to have some fun with our collective skills. Before we closed for the holiday break at the end of 2018, we made a fun microsite where users could leave a drawing in a book where everyone could see it. This project uses Node, Express, MongoDB, and Fabric.js for creating the draw on canvas experience. It was really neat to see what people were able to create in this minimal browser version of Illustrator.

Meet NYU

Meet NYU came to Athletics to create a fun and colorful hub for prospective and existing students. In 2019 we integrated a new WordPress them that allows their editorial team the flexibility to create content tailored to their content. We worked heavily with their accessbility team to audit build a new site for all students. In the following year, the pandemic hit and they needed to create a virtual campus experience. We created an accessbile campus map that can be created in the editor per campus on a tight timeline.
